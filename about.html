About

Lipid scientist.
Scientific partner.

PhD scientist with deep expertise in lipid biology, lipidomics, and omics data science. Based in Hamilton, Ontario. Working with clients in Canada, the United States, and Brazil.

I am a lipid scientist who built the analytical workflows to study lipids at scale — and learned to write the code to make sense of the data. That breadth — from lipid metabolism to LC-MS method development to statistical pipelines — is what makes the difference between data and insight.

That foundation shapes how I approach lipidomics data. Most lipidomics consultants come from the analytical chemistry or data science side and learned the biology later. I come from the biology — and developed the analytical and computational expertise to work with large-scale LC-MS datasets, build QC frameworks, and integrate omics data layers.

My most recent academic role at McMaster University involved large-scale LC-MS lipidomics on a nationally representative cross-sectional pediatric study — over 5,000 samples across 26 analytical batches. That project required designing and validating QC pipelines capable of handling population-scale biological complexity.

I work fluently in English and Portuguese — which allows me to serve clients and communicate science across different markets with equal depth and cultural fluency.
