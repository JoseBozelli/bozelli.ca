About

Lipid scientist.
Scientific partner.

PhD scientist with deep expertise in lipid biology, lipidomics, and omics data science. Based in Hamilton, Ontario. Working with clients in Canada, the United States, and Brazil.

Photo coming soon

My scientific background begins in the fundamental biology of lipids — their physical chemistry, membrane interactions, and roles in cellular signaling. I spent years investigating how lipids behave at the molecular level, how they mediate biological processes, and how disruptions in lipid biology connect to disease.

That foundation shapes how I approach lipidomics data. Most lipidomics consultants come from the analytical chemistry or data science side and learned the biology later. I come from the biology — and developed the analytical and computational expertise to work with large-scale LC-MS datasets, build QC frameworks, and integrate omics data layers.

My most recent work as a Research Associate at McMaster University involved large-scale LC-MS lipidomics on the ENANI-2019 cohort — a nationally representative pediatric study spanning over 5,000 samples and 26 analytical batches. That project required designing and validating QC pipelines capable of handling population-scale biological complexity.

I am also bilingual — working fluently in English and Portuguese — which allows me to serve clients and communicate science across the Canadian, American, and Brazilian markets with equal depth and cultural fluency.
